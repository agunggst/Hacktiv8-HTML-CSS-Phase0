Octagonal Emporium Gramophone

  • Brand: Emporium
  • Color: Brown
  • Size: Octagonal Standar (28")
  • Period: 18th Century, 19th Century
  • Material: Wood, Brass
  • Item code: IC-1016
  • Working condition: 78 rpm
  • Price: USD $3200
  • Weight: 17Kg

Octagonal emporium is another contemporary brand which was eventually swallowed up by HMV/Victor. Sometimes known as ‘Hornless’ gramophones, these were actually some of the first internal horn machines and this is a model 1 which was shown in their 1919/1920 model year catalogue. It has a manual brake, 10” turntable and a single spring motor (predictably the model 2 had a 12” turntable and a double spring motor!) I got this machine ridiculously cheaply on eBay but it was in awful condition. It had a later Decca soundbox on it and the wrong winding handle. The handle arm was so long, in fact, that you had to lift the unit up to wind it otherwise the handle would hit the table. It was old and dirty and needed a full restoration; the motor was completely stripped down and cleaned, re-assembled, adjusted and lubricated.
